GAMING FOR GOOD.

"Gaming for good" - a sentence that feels almost contradictory, considering how many people think that games are a net negative for humanity - but games have their good side as well! In this website, I'll explain exactly how gaming can have benefits, and isn't always bad!

The advantages

Games, especially educational games, have been proven to be beneficial, and in many ways. Here are some examples!

Focus and Memory

Games can help sharpen focus and improve memory retention through repeated play and engaging challenges.

Critical Thinking and Problem-Solving Skills

Many educational games require players to strategize, analyze, and adapt to challenges, fostering these essential skills.

Collaboration and Teamwork

Some games are designed for group play, encouraging students to work together, communicate, and share ideas.

Promotes Social and Emotional Learning (SEL)

Educational games can help promote social and emotional learning (SEL) by providing interactive experiences that help the development of key skills in children.

Recommended educational games

These educational games are some of the best gamified learning experiences, and are all great games - if you're looking to maybe learn something new or just for entertainment.

Duolingo

https://www.duolingo.com/

Duolingo is the most popular language-learning website on the internet. Duolingo turns language-learning into a game - in Duolingo, you have hearts, which go away if you make a mistake in any of their bite-sized exercises. It turns learning from a chore into something much more interactive and enjoyable!

Brilliant

https://brilliant.org

Brilliant is a website and mobile app in which you can learn many academic subjects, including maths, science, computer programming, and logic. Brilliant is quite a great website, in my opinion, with its comprehensive and enjoyable exercises. There's even a leaderboard and tiers on which you can compete!

PhET Lab

https://phet.colorado.edu/

PhET Lab, an application from the University of Colorado, is a physics-simulation web app used by hundreds of schools around the world to illustrate concepts in physics, mechanics, quantum phhysics, and so much more! It's very vaired and interactive, ensuring no limit to the fun, imagination, and creativity you can have!

Code Combat

https://codecombat.com/

CodeCombat is an online website that combines programming and game-based learning into one - you navigate through a dungeon, collect weapons, and defeat bosses all through the power of code! According to CodeCombat's team, you can "code your own shareable games"! It mainly focuses on Python and JavaScript.
